Predecessor & Successor

▼

The predecessor is the number that comes just before (subtract 1). The successor is the number that comes just after (add 1).

Watch how digits roll — especially at numbers like 1,00,000 and 9,99,999!

6

Roman Numerals

▼

Romans used letters to write numbers! Here are the 7 basic symbols:

Rules:

  • Additive: When a smaller or equal symbol comes after a larger one → ADD them. VI = 5 + 1 = 6
  • Subtractive: When a smaller symbol comes before a larger one → SUBTRACT. IV = 5 - 1 = 4
  • Repetition: A symbol can repeat up to 3 times. III = 3, but NOT IIII
  • Never repeat: V, L, D are never repeated

7

Greatest & Smallest Numbers

▼

To form the greatest number, arrange digits in descending order (largest first). For the smallest, arrange in ascending order — but remember, a number can't start with 0!

8

Rounding & Estimation

▼

Rounding helps us estimate! Look at the digit to the right of the place you're rounding to. If it's 5 or more, round up. If it's less than 5, round down.
