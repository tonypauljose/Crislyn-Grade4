What is a Noun?

▼

A noun is a naming word. It names a person, place, animal, thing, or idea.

Crislyn went to the park with her dog. She felt happiness as she played with a bunch of friends. The swings were made of iron.

Every coloured word above is a noun! Can you spot why?

2

5 Types of Nouns

▼
📦

Common Noun

General names for people, places, things

girl, city, table, river
⭐

Proper Noun

Specific names — always CAPITALISED

Crislyn, Bahrain, Monday
👥

Collective Noun

Names for a GROUP of things

flock, team, bunch, swarm
💭

Abstract Noun

Things you CANNOT see or touch

happiness, courage, freedom
🪨

Material Noun

Substances things are made FROM

gold, wood, cotton, glass

💡 Quick Test: Can you see it? Can you touch it?

  • If YES → it might be a common, proper, or material noun
  • If NO (it's a feeling/quality/idea) → it's an abstract noun
  • If it names a GROUP → it's a collective noun
  • If it starts with a CAPITAL letter (and isn't at the start of a sentence) → it's a proper noun
3

Singular & Plural Nouns

▼

Singular = one. Plural = more than one. Here are the rules:

Rule 1: Most nouns → add -s
cat → cats, book → books, pen → pens
Rule 2: Ends in s, sh, ch, x, o → add -es
bus → buses, dish → dishes, box → boxes, tomato → tomatoes
Rule 3: Consonant + y → change y to i, add -es
baby → babies, city → cities, story → stories
Rule 4: Vowel + y → just add -s
boy → boys, key → keys, toy → toys
Rule 5: Ends in f or fe → change to -ves
leaf → leaves, knife → knives, wolf → wolves
Irregular! No rule — memorise these:
child → children, man → men, woman → women, mouse → mice, tooth → teeth, foot → feet, goose → geese, ox → oxen
Same singular & plural:
sheep → sheep, deer → deer, fish → fish
4

Countable & Uncountable Nouns

▼

✅ Countable

Can be counted: one apple, two apples, three apples

Use: many, few, a few, several, a number of

Examples: books, chairs, eggs, pencils, dogs

⚠️ Uncountable

Cannot be counted individually: water, rice, sugar

Use: much, little, a little, a lot of, some

Examples: water, milk, bread, furniture, information, homework

Common Mistake: Never say "many furnitures" or "two homeworks" or "three informations"!
5

Possessive Nouns

▼

Possessive nouns show ownership — who something belongs to. We use an apostrophe (').

Singular noun → add 's
the girl's book, the dog's bone, Crislyn's pencil
Plural ending in -s → add just '
the boys' team, the teachers' room, the students' bags
Plural NOT ending in -s → add 's
the children's toys, the men's shoes, the women's club
