1

What is a Sentence?

▼

A sentence is a group of words that makes complete sense. Every sentence has two parts:

Subject

Who or what the sentence is about.

The doer of the action.

Predicate

What the subject does or is.

The action or description.

Crislyn rides her bicycle every evening.

The big brown dog chased the ball across the garden.

My favourite book is about a magical world.

Blue underline = Subject, Pink underline = Predicate

Not a sentence: "Running in the park." (No subject — WHO is running?)  |  "The tall girl." (No predicate — what about her?)
2

The Four Types of Sentences

▼

Every sentence does one of four things. Let's learn each type!

📢

Declarative

Makes a statement or tells a fact.

.
"Crislyn goes to school in Bahrain."
❓

Interrogative

Asks a question.

?
"Where is your school?"
😲

Exclamatory

Shows strong feeling or emotion.

!
"What a beautiful day it is!"
👉

Imperative

Gives a command or request.

. or !
"Please close the door."
3

Declarative Sentences (Statements)

▼

A declarative sentence tells something. It shares a fact, opinion, or piece of information. It always ends with a full stop (.)

"The Earth revolves around the Sun." Fact
"I like playing with Legos." Opinion
"Bahrain is an island country in the Middle East." Information
"She does not like rainy days." Negative statement
Key Signs: Tells you something. Ends with a full stop (.). Can be positive or negative.
4

Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

▼

An interrogative sentence asks a question. It always ends with a question mark (?)

"What is your name?" Wh- question
"Did you finish your homework?" Yes/No question
"Where are you going after school?" Wh- question
"Can I borrow your pencil?" Yes/No question
Key Signs: Asks something. Ends with a question mark (?). Often starts with: Who, What, Where, When, Why, How, Is, Are, Do, Did, Can, Will.
5

Exclamatory Sentences (Strong Feelings)

▼

An exclamatory sentence shows strong emotion — surprise, joy, anger, excitement, or fear. It ends with an exclamation mark (!)

"What a wonderful surprise!" Surprise
"How beautiful this garden is!" Admiration
"I can't believe we won the match!" Excitement
"Ouch! That really hurts!" Pain
Key Signs: Shows strong feeling. Ends with an exclamation mark (!). Often starts with: What, How, or an interjection (Wow, Ouch, Hurray).
Tricky! Not every sentence with "!" is exclamatory. "Stop right there!" is imperative (a command), not exclamatory. The difference? Exclamatory expresses emotion, imperative gives an order.
6

Imperative Sentences (Commands & Requests)

▼

An imperative sentence gives a command, order, instruction, or request. It usually ends with a full stop (.) but can end with ! for strong commands.

"Please open your books to page 42." Polite request
"Sit down and be quiet." Command
"Mix the flour and sugar together." Instruction
"Don't touch the hot stove!" Warning
Key Signs: Tells someone to DO something. The subject is usually hidden ("you" is understood). Often starts with a verb (Open, Close, Write, Don't...) or "Please".
Hidden Subject: "Close the door." → Who should close it? You (understood). The full sentence is "(You) close the door." but we don't say "you" in imperative sentences!
